What is salami?

Salami has its name because of the process in which it is made. Salare, Italian: "to salt". Salami is actually just the plural term for salame, meaning salted meat, but currently refers to a very specific type of salted meat. It consists of fermented and air-dried meat, typically pork or beef.
The origin of salami is not known, since a variety of different european countries and cultures have their own local version.
